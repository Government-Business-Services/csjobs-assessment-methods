v.04

Journey A:

VH selects a job-library template

The usual campaign set-up prototype with a fast-tracked journey after the vacancy holder opts to use a job template. Assessment methods are summarised for a review by the vacancy holder (task-list steps are 'completed').

Journey B:

VH answers 'triage' questions to obtain recommendations

Assessment methods are recommended in response to 'triage' question answered by the vacancy holder. This journey is not integrated into the campaign set-up structure, this is to allow the experiment to focus on the questions and recommending aspects only.

Overarching hypothesis

Whichever way you look at it, the choice of selection criteria and assessment methods is indelibly connected with all aspects of Civil Service recruitment, end to end and front to back. Given this, we shouldn’t be surprised to learn how far reaching its impact is felt. Take, for example, the job library concept and job templates, or how reserve list matching works, or, possibly most significantly, the flavour of journey and experience offered to candidates if they wish to join the service.
